About

I’m Shreyas, and I study Math with Computer Science (18C) at MIT. In my free time, I like reading, playing tennis, hiking, and occasionally gaming.

You can ask what makes me me, and I’d argue the best way to give you a sense is by describing what I find cool. What makes my heart race, what bubbles the blood in my veins, what gets the neurons firing until I don’t sleep at night.

I’m enthused by words with structure and rhythm. I like music that rather then surfing old tides creates waves in its wake, with lyrics that mean something or acoustics that ebb and swell to give a Daft Punk-esque pulse of layered emotion. I look for movies that get the adrenaline pumping, that compel my chest to heave and my head to thump backwards in mirth, or that coerce my eyes to glisten over in a carefully concealed congealing of tears. I place drama infinitely above the farce that is comedy.

But I’m only rambling, and you want something more concrete.


School

I enjoy student life. MIT has expanded my horizons, and I’ve made friends who, apart from being brilliant, quirky, and creatively infuriating, are straight up some of the most awesome people I’ve met.

What about coursework, you ask? Well, this semester, I’m taking Complex Analysis (18.212), Quantum Mechanics II (8.05), Advanced Algorithms (G) (6.854), Machine Learning (G) (6.867), and Reading Fiction (21L.003). It’s a problem-set heavy courseload, but I’m very excited about the content. My favorite four classes I’ve completed at MIT so far are Intro Communication Networks (6.02), (Abstract) Algebra I (18.701), Algebraic Combinatorics (18.212), and Inference and Information (6.437).


Internships

This summer, I interned as a Quantitative Developer at Seven Eight Capital. It’s a small but rapidly growing company, and I found my time there a rewarding experience. It’s nice to work in a close-knit team and deploy projects with direct relevance to what everyone is working on.

I also found New York City a vibrant and amazing place in general. I enjoyed exploring the city (but, as a New York Times reader, I finally related to the subway bashing that I see periodically). I went to a few Broadway shows and still find myself reeling in disbelief that humans can be so talented.
